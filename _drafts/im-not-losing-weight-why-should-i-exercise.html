Cardio burns >40% of the muscle fat, doubles lipolytic protein expression and increases the total oxidative capacity of obese men within 8 weeks.

All subjects started with 20min of cycling or running at only 30% of their VO2max and were encouraged to try to build up to 20-60min of exercise at a maximal intensity of 85% of the VO2-max.

How much weight did 102kg guys lose? Not a single pound! But this is not about losing weight - to do that you would use your diet (!), not your workouts - training is about building the engine to burn fat and that worked quite effectively with a protocol that was frequent (5 sessions per week), and progressive.www.suppversity.com

Reference: Louche K, Badin PM, Montastier E, Laurens C, Bourlier V, de Glisezinski I, Thalamas C, Viguerie N, Langin D, Moro C. Endurance Exercise Training Up-Regulates Lipolytic Proteins and Reduces Triglyceride Content in Skeletal Muscle of Obese Subjects. J Clin Endocrinol Metab. 2013 Oct 31. [Epub ahead of print]

 

 

Effect of prior exercise on postprandial lipemia: HIIT > Cardio > Strength training

A soon-to-be-published review in the Journal of Physiology says:

+ women benefit more from working out before a meal
+ HIIT has the greatest benefit on postprandial triglyceride levels
+ being in a coloric deficit doubles the effect size

With less than 1/10 of the effect of HIIT and a 4.5x lower effect size than 'regular cardio' strength training alone (!) is not really suited for someone who wants to keep his blood lipids in check. www.suppversity.com

Reference: Freese et al. Effect of prior exercise on postprandial lipemia: an updated quantitative review. Journal of Applied Physiology November 7, 2013 jap.00623.2013
