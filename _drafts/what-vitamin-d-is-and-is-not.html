In a previous post, I have tried to address some of the myths around Vitamin-D and especially that pertaining to Calcium.

Vitamin D binding protein and kidney cancer - 83% reduced risk with high, not low levels

It's funny, only a couple of days ago I told you about the beneficial metabolic effects (glucose and lipid metabolism) of FREE vitamin D and how this may render all previous studies that measured total vitamin D, only, meaningless.

Sugested Read: "Underestimated Vitamin D Sources: Especially Eggs, But Also Chicken, Pork, Fish & Dairy Contain an Overlooked, Physiologically Relevant Amount of Ready-Made 25OHD" @ www.suppversity.com/2013/11/underestimated-vitamin-d-sources.html

Today, I have to add that at your kidney is going to be happier with HIGH vitamin D binging proteins (and thus low free vitamin D) and low free vitamin D [you could argue that this is logical as high active vitamin D levels entail the risk of kidney calcification; if more D is bound, this problem should be reduced]

That's at least what the significant negative association between vitamin D binding protein and kidney cancer scientists from the National Cancer Institute found, when they re-analyzed data from the Alpha-Tocopherol, Beta-Carotene Cancer Prevention (ATBC) Study, would suggest. www.suppversity.com

Reference: Modul AM, et al. Vitamin D binding protein, circulating vitamin D, and risk of renal cell carcinoma. International Journal of Cancer. November 2013 [accepted manuscript]
