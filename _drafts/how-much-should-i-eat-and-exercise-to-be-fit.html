There is a whole bunch of superstition around the correct constitution of diets (low carb, high carb, no carb), timing (dont eat at night, eat 10 times a day), etc. When it comes to exercise programs, there is even more confusion and gurus to go around - your friendly neighborhood gym trainer foremost amongst them.

This article does not attempt to touch those topics - it actually answers something far more simpler and quantifiable: How much to eat/exercise.

Now, why is this important ? Hasnt your friendly neighborhood dietician asked you to eat your cucumbers and almonds and stop counting calories ? or eat 1 litre of ghee and dont bother counting ?

The answer takes us all the way from Gary Taubes to the GOMAD (Gallon of Milk a Day) diet to 2-DEAD (Two Dozen Eggs A Day) diet and back again.

The answer is about insulin, leptin and exercise.

the EPOC is by far not the only metabolic effect that's missing from the "standard equation of exercise induced weight loss", i.e. [calorie intake - (resting metabolic rate + calories burnt during exercise)] / [3,500 kcal/lbs] = amount of fat lost (in lbs). In fact that is evident in the HIIT article on this site, where I mention  the Rosenkilde study (of increased aerobic volume not really losing more weight)
